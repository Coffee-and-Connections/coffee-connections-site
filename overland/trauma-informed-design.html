Property Stewardship Standards

The Architecture of Stability

Transforming 216 E Overland through Trauma-Informed Design.

01

Clear Sightlines

By eliminating blind corners in the lobby and residential hallways, we reduce tenant hyper-vigilance. This architectural choice increases passive safety and reduces the need for intrusive security presence.

02

Choice & Control

Our floor plans prioritize tenant autonomy. Giving residents control over their immediate environment—from lighting to seating—is proven to reduce behavioral incidents and increase long-term occupancy.

03

Acoustic Engineering

We utilize sound-dampening materials to mitigate the "echo-chamber" effect common in industrial buildings. This supports sensory-friendly study for UTEP students and peace for upper-floor residents.

04

Biophilic Integration

Natural light and greenery are clinical interventions. They lower cortisol levels, making the building a preferred destination for high-end café customers and long-term residents alike.

05

The Trost Legacy & The Boiler

Our design honors the 1920s Trost & Trost architecture. We aren't just renovating; we are preserving. The historic boiler in the basement will be restored as an industrial centerpiece for our 7,000 sq ft community game zone—anchoring the building's past to its resilient future.

The Bottom Line: A trauma-informed building is a stable building. By investing in these design principles, we ensure 216 E Overland becomes a flagship for modern, mixed-use reintegration in the El Paso Borderplex.
