The Architecture of Stability

Transforming 216 E Overland through Trauma-Informed Design.

01

Clear Sightlines

By eliminating blind corners in the lobby and residential hallways, we reduce tenant hyper-vigilance. This architectural choice increases safety and reduces the need for intrusive security presence.

02

Choice & Control

Our floor plans prioritize tenant autonomy. Giving residents control over their immediate environment—from lighting to seating—drastically reduces property damage and behavioral incidents.

03

Acoustic Engineering

We utilize sound-dampening materials to mitigate the "echo-chamber" effect of historic buildings. This keeps ambient noise at a level that supports focus for students and peace for residents.

04

Biophilic Integration

Natural light and greenery aren't just for looks. They are proven to lower cortisol levels, making the building a preferred destination for both high-end café customers and long-term residents.

The Bottom Line: A trauma-informed building is a stable building. By investing in these design principles, we ensure 216 E Overland becomes a flagship for modern, mixed-use reintegration.
