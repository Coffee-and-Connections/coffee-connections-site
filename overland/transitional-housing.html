The Bridge @ Overland

Architectural Choice as a Clinical Intervention.

01

The Runway to Resilience

The Bridge @ Overland serves as an Active Reintegration Runway. By utilizing the upper floors of the Lenox Building, we are creating a high-support living environment supported by HUD and State assistance. This sanctuary is designed to be a low-friction, secure harbor where survivors of trauma can stabilize their surroundings and gather the momentum needed to launch into permanent, independent housing.

  • Goal-Oriented Stays: Focused on short-to-medium term stabilization (1-6 months) to ensure a successful launch.
  • HUD & State Funded: Leveraging existing government vouchers to remove financial barriers while residents rebuild.
  • Trauma-Informed Layout: Using clear sightlines and acoustic dampening to actively lower cortisol and support neurodivergent recovery.
02

Professional Handshake & Advocacy

True stabilization requires resolving the complex "weights" that lead to housing insecurity. We connect our residents with an elite network of on-site professional partners to ensure their next move is their last move.

  • Legal Restoration: Direct connection to partner attorneys for legal advocacy, discharge upgrades, and civil stability.
  • Financial Fortitude: Mandatory sessions with certified financial planners to build a sustainable "Launch Budget."
  • Vocational Growth: Immediate entry into the Coffee & Connections training engine on the ground floor to build professional capital.
