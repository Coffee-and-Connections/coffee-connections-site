01

Operational Infrastructure

Leveraging 19 years of US Army logistical expertise, our CEO Anthony Sorensen has designed a housing model that functions with 100% accountability. This isn't just a building; it's a high-reliability communications hub for recovery.

  • Accountability: $30M+ asset management mindset applied to human safety.
  • Sightlines: Architectural design that eliminates "blind spots" to reduce survivor hypervigilance.
  • Sustainability: Integrated with the Coffee & Connections economic engine.
02

The Survivor-First Environment

Led by Marylou Culpepper, the housing protocols are built on the 'Warm Handoff.' We ensure that MST survivors and junior soldiers have a designated voice in their living environment, protecting human dignity above all else.

  • The Umbrella Protocol: Wrap-around care that follows the resident from the street to the suite.
  • Sensory Regulation: Acoustic dampening and lighting designed for neurodivergent recovery.
  • Peer Leadership: Resident-led "Rally Points" within the living quarters.
