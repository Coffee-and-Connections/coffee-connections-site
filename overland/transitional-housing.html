Upper Floor Development Model

The Bridge @ Overland

A Reintegration Runway to Permanent Independence.

01

Short-Term Stabilization Plan

The Bridge @ Overland is designed as an Active Reintegration Runway. We are transforming the top two floors into a high-support living environment funded through HUD and State rental assistance programs. This sanctuary is designed to be a low-friction, secure harbor where survivors can stabilize their surroundings and gather the momentum needed to transition into permanent, independent housing.

  • The Runway Model: Stays are goal-oriented (typically 1 to 6 months) to ensure residents are moving toward independent living, not permanent residency.
  • HUD/State Funding: We leverage government housing vouchers and grants to ensure all rent is covered while residents focus on stabilization.
  • Safe Sightlines: Architectural layout is designed to eliminate blind corners and reduce survivor hypervigilance.
02

Wrap-Around Professional Services

Stabilization requires more than just a bed. We connect our residents with an elite network of on-site professional partners to resolve the issues that lead to housing instability.

  • Legal Restoration: Direct access to partner attorneys to resolve civil legal issues, veteran discharge upgrades, or advocacy.
  • Financial Resilience: Certified planners help residents manage their state vouchers and build a sustainable "Launch Budget."
  • Vocational Synergy: Residents have immediate access to the Coffee & Connections vocational training engine on the ground floor.
