Where It Started

It started in a car. Tony had been thinking about what life might look like after the Army. His love of coffee sparked an idea: “I should open a coffee shop.”

One day, on the way to or from an intensive group trauma therapy appointment, Marylou said: “What if you tied your coffee shop to mental health?” A simple sentence. And an idea was born.

What if we took all the pain—all the missed opportunities where someone could've helped us—and turned that original idea into a bridge? A space that caught people before the crisis. A space where saying “I'm not okay” wasn’t a liability... but an opening.

A place where someone who's walked through the fire says: “You're not alone. We got you.” Even if that means just sitting with them. Listening. Or helping them find the help they need right now.

We're not building a coffee shop. We're building the space we wished we had when we needed it most.
